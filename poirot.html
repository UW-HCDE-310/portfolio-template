Poirot

A web inspector for designers

Summary

Not all web designers enjoy using Chrome DevTools or other web inspectors; Poirot is a Chrome extension that allows users to make local changes to a live website via a GUI. I was a co-author on our paper that compared tools designers currently have and what tools they would like to have.

Timeline: August 2018-May 2019

Outline

  1. The problem
  2. Solution
  3. Impact

The problem

During a formative investigation consisting of interviews, a survey, and an analysis of professional design documents, my team explored the issues designers face as they interact with developers and developer tools. My team found that many designers were more comfortable with graphical interfaces than they were with Chrome DevTools and other web inspectors.

Solution

My collaborators developed Poirot, a web inspection tool for designers that enables designers to make style edits with buttons and drop down menus instead of editing CSS attributes.

Impact

We compared Poirot to Chrome DevTools in a lab study with 16 design professionals. We observed common problems designers experience when using Chrome DevTools and found that when using Poirot, designers were more successful in accomplishing typical design tasks (97% to 63%). In addition, we found that Poirot had a significantly lower perceived cognitive load and was overwhelmingly preferred by the designers in our study.

I read over 50 research papers and wrote the background research section of our paper. I am second author on our CHI '19 paper.
