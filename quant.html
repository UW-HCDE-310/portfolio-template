Quantitative research

I love using Python and working with data! Here are some of my recent hobby projects where I use public datasets, analyze, and visualize them to answer questions I have.

Research questions
  1. What trends are there in crime in Seattle, WA? Are Ballard and Pioneer Square neighborhoods really getting "more dangerous"?
  2. How is the ratio of minority gender students changing in graduates of bachelor degrees like computer science and other STEM programs?
  3. How is "the job market" changing in the US? In particular, I'm interested in the number of openings, the location (remote, hybrid, in person), the number of applicants, and how long the listing is up?
1. Crime in Seattle

Research questions and hypothesis

Folks in Seattle tell newcomers the same information that they were told when they moved to Seattle. But do the numbers back the claims that are shared so often?



Hypothesis Plan
The neighborhoods with the most reported crimes are: South Park, Rainier Beach, Othello, Beacon Hill, Burien, White Center, Skyway, and Yesler. Calculate mean and standard deviation for reported crimes in each neighborhood. Conduct a hypothesis test (one-way ANOVA or non-parametric test) to determine if the difference is statistically significant. Calculate a confidence interval to see if the reported crimes overlap.
Some types of crime are increasing in Ballard and Pioneer Square. Hypothesis test (chi-square or fisher's exact test) and regression analysis.
Crime has increased around the new Link stations after they opened. According to Wikipedia, Northgate, Roosevelt, and U district stations opened on October 2, 2021; Angel Lake opened on September 24, 2016; both the Capitol Hill and University of Washington stations opened on March 19, 2014). Calculate crimes per month the year before and after each station opened. Conduct a hypothesis test (t-test, a z-test, or Wilcoxon rank-sum test) and regression analysis.
The stations with the highest ridership have the most crime in the neighborhood. According to Wikipedia, that would be Westlake (12594), UW (11,200), Capitol Hill (8,408), Chinatown (7,461), University Street (6,241), SeaTac Airport(5,640), Pioneer Square (4,764). Calculate a correlation coefficient (Pearson's or Spearman's) and perform a regression analysis. Plot the number of riders and the number of reported crimes in the neighborhood surrounding each station in a scatter plot.
Most reports of prostitution happen along Aurora Ave Conduct hypothesis test (chi-square test or a Fisher's exact test). Comparing mean and standard deviation of the number of reported incidents too.
Violent crimes are increasing near the University. Hypothesis test (t-tests or ANOVA) and compare the mean and standard deviation of the number of reported incidents.

Gather and clean data

I downloaded a *.csv of the SPD Crime Data: 2008-Present data the last week of April 2023.



Overall, I was very impressed with how clean the data was. Some events were reported with the latitude and longitude (0,0), so I removed those, as well as a few outliers that were not in the county, let alone Seattle City limits. Next, I had to convert the dates from strings to datetime format, but I needed to drop the nan values first.
