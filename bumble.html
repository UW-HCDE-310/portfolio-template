Bumble BFF

This app that allows users to swipe on profiles, similar to a dating app, but to create meaningful friendships. As a personal project (I have no affiliation with Bumble), I scoped research questions, designed and ran ten user interviews, and analyzed the data in order to understand how women in their 20s make friends 1:1 and in group settings and a explore a hypothetical group activity feature.

Role

Company

Timeline

Teammates

Key research questions
  1. How do people make friends on and off Bumble?
    1. On Bumble, are there other ways people 'hack' the system? (eg, put IG or snapchat handles in bios, group meetups, etc)
  2. What does/does not work well with Bumble and other ways people make friends?
    1. What variables influence what activities seem like they will work/actually will work?
  3. How would group meetups be different from 1:1 hangouts?
    1. Would they solve or cause pain points?
    2. Would they feel less like dates? More natural or comfortable?
    3. Would they help users make more connections or friendships?
    4. Would they help users feel more comfortable matching and meeting people? People of different races/genders/sexual identities?
    5. Would users be more or less interested in group meetups or 1:1 meetups?

Study methodology
Participants

I recruited four participants who were active Bumble users, three participants who were inactive users, and three who had never used Bumble.

Interviews

User interviews are a fast way to learn about users and what they think, feel, and need. I conducted 10 in-depth 1:1 structured user interviews using this moderator's guide.
